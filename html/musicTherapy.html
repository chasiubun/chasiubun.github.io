What is Music Therapy?

According to the American Music Therapy Association:

Music Therapy is the clinical & evidence-based use of music interventions to accomplish individualized goals within a therapeutic relationship by a credentialed professional who has completed an approved music therapy program. Music therapy interventions can address a variety of healthcare & educational goals:

  • Promote Wellness
  • Manage Stress
  • Alleviate Pain
  • Express Feelings
  • Enhance Memory
  • Improve Communication
  • Promote Physical Rehabilitation
  • and more
(American Music Therapy Association, 2005)

Examples of the Use of Music in a Therapeutic Manner in the Bible:

One of the earliest examples of the use of music as medicine in history comes from 1 Samuel 16:14-23. When King Saul was tormented by an evil spirit, he could only be comforted by David, the man of faith. Saul was "refreshed and well."

God also tells us in his word that music is a source of teaching (Col 3:16-17) in corporate times of worship. The book of Psalms themselves possess a treasury of truth expressed through the various experiences of saints from joys to sorrows. The Psalms give the Christian a means for rejoicing, lamentation, remembrance, fellowship, and hope. They feature expressions of faith and love. Many of these Psalms serve as methods for prayer and dependence.
